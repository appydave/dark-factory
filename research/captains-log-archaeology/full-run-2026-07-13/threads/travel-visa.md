## Timeline

### 2026-02-21 — STARTED: the deadline enters the record
A café conversation in Chiang Mai (mostly about AI wearables and dictation) carries the seed of this thread: David notes the Thai visa mechanics — 60/30-day extensions, 90-day reporting — and the hard constraint that he must **exit Thailand by March 24** to satisfy his six-month stay limit. The thread begins as ambient awareness, not yet a plan.

### 2026-03-04 — EVOLVED: the border-run burst (8 captures, one day)
Eleven days later the deadline converts into intense same-day research and a rapid sequence of decisions that revise each other within hours:

1. **Morning research + first decision** — a friend confirms **Chong Chom** (Thai–Cambodia border) is the open crossing; David decides on a guided border-run service at **~2,000–2,500 THB** (with an optional ~3,200 THB immigration-assistance add-on) rather than going solo. *(⚠️ SUPERSEDED later the same day — both the crossing choice and the price tier.)*
2. **Network activation** — he works his contacts: an acquaintance whose DTV colleague just did a run (bad audio → 08:00 phone call scheduled); an experienced expat friend (tourist→school→retirement visa arc) who promises contact details and mentions a "Schumann" who may still organise runs.
3. **Vetting heuristic captured** — David sets his standing rule for any Thai visa agent: *ask how they handle and mitigate problems with immigration* before trusting them. This is the durable learning of the day.
4. **Price confusion probed** — a phone call to a visa service surfaces a different structure: **4,500 THB + 45 USD at the border**, conflicting with the earlier 2,000–2,500 figure. David probes the 2,000-vs-4,500 split and failure modes.
5. **Final decision (supersedes #1)** — at the bus terminal with Joy, David commits to the **hand-held same-day run via Tanisha Imran's small-car service (~4,500 THB + $50 USD note, no forms)** over the cheaper overnight Green Bus (~1,000–2,500 THB), explicitly optimising for *minimised immigration anxiety* over cost. He also agrees to write up the experience for his friend's blog.

**Superseded on 2026-03-04**: (a) Chong Chom solo-ish cheap guided run at 2,000–2,500 THB → replaced by the premium 4,500 THB same-day hand-held service; (b) the overnight-bus option → rejected for same-day.

### 2026-03-18 — EVOLVED + quiet pivot: the run becomes a LAOS run
Two weeks later, night-before-departure captures show the destination has **silently switched from Cambodia (Chong Chom) to Laos** — the Mar-04 crossing decision is superseded without an explicit revocation record. Pre-trip state:

- **Contingency planning**: travel light; if denied re-entry, someone brings extra items. Research confirms the Thai border pass is residency-limited (unavailable to Chiang Mai residents), so any companion needs a passport — **Joy has none**; a friend with a valid passport is the identified backup.
- **Packing list**: 3 shirts/underwear/socks, 2 glasses, laptop, microphone, CPAP, **$300 USD + 5,000 THB**, adapters, cards; verify online banking before departure.
- **Post-trip admin queued**: file the **TM30** on return; and "Joanne needs a passport" (almost certainly Joy — the passport gap is the same one blocking her as border companion).

The run itself is not captured, but downstream evidence (TM30-on-return framing, later dtv-brain activity) indicates it happened on/around the March 24 deadline.
